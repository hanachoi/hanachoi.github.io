---
title: "How AI-Generated Review Summaries Shape Consumer Beliefs, Purchases, and Product Returns"
co-authors: "with Minkyung Kim and Kevin Lee"
category: work in progress
---

AI is increasingly serving as an information layer between consumers and markets, transforming how product information is synthesized and delivered. As AI summarizes reviews, compares products, and recommends alternatives, a fundamental question arises: how does AI-mediated information shape consumers' beliefs, purchase decisions, and post-purchase outcomes? <br><br>
This project proposes an identification strategy to infer consumers' beliefs by jointly observing purchase and return behaviors in response to changes in product reviews. We then apply this framework to study the large-scale adoption of AI-generated review summaries across more than 1,000 e-commerce websites. Leveraging variation before and after adoption, together with differences in information complexity and valence emphasis, we examine how AI-generated summaries shape consumer beliefs, product demand, and returns. The findings inform the design of AI-generated review summaries across retail settings.
